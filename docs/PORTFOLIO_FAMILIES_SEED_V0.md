# ELARA ZERO-OPS / DFN-0 — Portfolio Families Seed v0

## Purpose
Provide an initial family map for the broader Elara–Claudio portfolio so the synaptic mesh can be applied to real project clusters.

## Family 1 — AI Core, Orchestration, and Cognitive Infrastructure
Examples:
- orchestration and control frameworks
- memory and registry systems
- multi-AI routing
- synaptic mesh
- local node and fallback profile

## Family 2 — Semiconductors, Compute, and Advanced Chips
Examples:
- semiconductor and fab-related programs
- post-2nm and beyond-CMOS research directions
- advanced compute stack and related infrastructure

## Family 3 — Software Runtime, Tools, and Digital Products
Examples:
- Ops Console v0
- software prototypes
- technical dashboards
- monetizable digital systems
- lightweight web runtime projects

## Family 4 — Robotics, Humanoids, and Mechatronic Systems
Examples:
- humanoid systems
- robotic support units
- embodied AI concepts
- mechatronic execution layers

## Family 5 — Mobility, Aerospace, and Vehicle Platforms
Examples:
- air mobility concepts
- ground mobility systems
- maritime and integrated platform concepts
- vehicle AI and infrastructure interaction programs

## Family 6 — Energy, Grid, and Power Systems
Examples:
- energy programs
- storage and grid integration projects
- alternative energy research tracks
- energy operating systems

## Family 7 — Manufacturing, Industrialization, and Physical Infrastructure
Examples:
- digital lab
- distributed fabrication network
- industrial preparation
- future manufacturing packets and supply-chain skeletons

## Family 8 — Medical, Biotech, Regeneration, and Longevity
Examples:
- medical systems
- biotech and regeneration concepts
- health-supporting product/platform ideas

## Family 9 — Built Environment, Habitats, and Operational Spaces
Examples:
- housing and interior-design programs
- resilient physical spaces
- operational base and facility concepts

## Family 10 — Commerce, Media, and Knowledge Products
Examples:
- websites, content systems, premium knowledge products, structured documentation, and market-facing information platforms

## Rule
Each portfolio project should eventually map to:
- one primary family
- one or more secondary families when needed
- one primary gap
- one readiness score
- one next highest-value step
